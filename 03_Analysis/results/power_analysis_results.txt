Post-hoc Power Analysis: Perioperative Oral Care Study
=================================================================
N = 47 prefectures, alpha = 0.05

--- Observed R² Based Power ---
  Model 1 (Unadjusted, k=1)
    R² = 0.0089, Cohen's f² = 0.0090
    Post-hoc power = 9.7%

  Model 2 (Adjusted,   k=4)
    R² = 0.1967, Cohen's f² = 0.2449
    Post-hoc power = 73.0%

--- Power by Effect Size at N=47, k=4 (Model 2) ---
  Small (f²=0.02): power = 9.6%
  Medium (f²=0.15): power = 49.2%
  Large  (f²=0.35): power = 88.4%

--- N Required for 80% Power (k=4) ---
  Small (f²=0.02): N needed = 999 [exceeds 47 prefectures]
  Medium (f²=0.15): N needed = 85 [exceeds 47 prefectures]
  Large  (f²=0.35): N needed = 40

--- Manuscript Limitations (suggested text) ---

Post-hoc power analysis using a non-central F distribution
(alpha = 0.05) indicated that with N = 47 prefectures and k = 4 predictors,
the adjusted model (R2 = 0.197; Cohen's f2 = 0.245) achieved
approximately 73% power — below the conventional 80% threshold.
For a medium effect size (f2 = 0.15), approximately N = 85
administrative units would be required to achieve 80% power,
which exceeds Japan's fixed unit of 47 prefectures.
Accordingly, the null finding should be interpreted as absence of
a detectable association at this ecological resolution rather than
proof of no association; Type II error cannot be excluded.
